# Sprecher East — Brand Voice Guidelines

> "Just neighbors helping neighbors."

---

## The Short Version

We sound like the neighbor who brings you tomatoes from their garden and also shows up at every city council meeting. We are warm, direct, a little bit fired up, and always real. If it sounds like it came from a committee, rewrite it. If it sounds like something you'd actually say on your front porch — ship it.

---

## Voice Pillars

### 1. Real Talk

Honest. Direct. Zero corporate speak.

We don't hide behind jargon or passive voice. We say what we mean. We talk like humans who live here — because we do. If a pothole has been eating tires on Sprecher Road for three months, we say that. We don't say "infrastructure challenges have been identified."

**Sounds like:** "Here's the deal." / "Let's be real." / "No sugarcoating."

### 2. Warm Energy

Enthusiastic without being fake.

We genuinely love this neighborhood and it shows. But we are not a pep rally — we are your neighbor who's excited about the block party AND about fixing the drainage issue on Trillium Way. Our energy comes from caring, not from marketing.

**Sounds like:** "We're so glad you're here!" / "This is going to be good." / "Come as you are."

### 3. Assertive Heart

We stand up for our neighborhood — firmly and respectfully.

We advocate. We push back when something isn't right. We show up to meetings, we write letters, we make calls. But we do it with respect, with facts, and with the understanding that the people on the other side of the table are people too. Assertive, never aggressive. Firm, never rude.

**Sounds like:** "We deserve better and here's why." / "We're not going to let this slide." / "Our neighbors need this — let's make it happen."

### 4. Empowering Action

We make neighbors feel capable, not dependent.

Every piece of content should leave someone feeling like they can do something. We don't just inform — we activate. We don't just complain — we organize. The goal is always: after reading this, you know what to do next.

**Sounds like:** "Here's how you can help." / "You don't need permission — just show up." / "Three things you can do right now."

## Do's and Don'ts

| Do                                                                           | Don't                                                |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Write like you're texting a friendly neighbor (with slightly better grammar) | Write like you're drafting a municipal ordinance     |
| Use contractions — "we're," "you'll," "let's"                                | Sound stiff — "we are pleased to announce"           |
| Name specific streets, parks, and landmarks                                  | Be vague — "our area" or "the community"             |
| Say "we" — we're in this together                                            | Say "the association" like it's a distant entity     |
| Get to the point in the first sentence                                       | Bury the news under three paragraphs of context      |
| Use active voice — "We planted 40 trees"                                     | Use passive voice — "40 trees were planted"          |
| Show personality and humor when appropriate                                  | Be so professional that nobody feels anything        |
| Acknowledge problems honestly                                                | Pretend everything is perfect                        |
| Tell people exactly what to do next                                          | Leave readers wondering "okay, so now what?"         |
| Be transparent about AI assistance                                           | Pretend AI-generated content is purely human-written |

## Tone by Context

Our voice stays consistent. Our tone shifts to match the moment.

### Welcoming New Neighbors

**Tone:** Warm, excited, zero pressure.

> "Hey, welcome to Sprecher East! Whether you just moved in or you've been here for years and are just now finding us — we're glad you're here. No dues, no obligations, no secret handshake. Just neighbors who look out for each other. Grab a seat, stay a while."

### Reporting Neighborhood Issues

**Tone:** Factual, direct, solution-oriented.

> "Heads up — the streetlight at Sprecher and Pflaum has been out for two weeks. We've reported it to the city (ticket #4821). If you've noticed it too, adding your voice helps. Here's the link to file a report — takes 30 seconds."

### Advocating to City Government

**Tone:** Firm, fact-based, respectful, persistent.

> "We appreciate the city's attention to east-side development, but Sprecher East has been left out of the conversation. Our neighborhood has grown 35% in the last five years. We need sidewalks, we need bus service, and we need a seat at the planning table. We've submitted our formal request and we'll keep showing up until we're heard."

### Event Promotions

**Tone:** Fun, energetic, low-barrier.

> "Neighborhood cookout this Saturday, 4 PM at Sprecher East Park. Burgers, brats, and whatever mystery dish Dave brings (last time it was good, we promise). Bring a lawn chair and your appetite. Kids, dogs, and new neighbors extra welcome."

### Responding to Criticism

**Tone:** Open, honest, non-defensive.

> "Fair point — we hear you. We should have communicated the meeting change earlier, and we'll do better next time. If you want to help us get the word out faster, we'd honestly love the help. Shoot us a message."

### Social Media Posts

**Tone:** Punchy, visual, action-forward.

> "That sunset over the prairie path last night? Unreal. This is why we live here. Drop your favorite Sprecher East view in the comments."

> "City budget hearing is Thursday at 6 PM. Sprecher East needs to be in that room. Who's coming with us? Link in bio."

## Word Choices

We swap out stiff, bureaucratic language for words real people actually use.

| Instead of...                   | Say...                                                              |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "The association"               | "We" or "Sprecher East"                                             |
| "Residents of the neighborhood" | "Neighbors"                                                         |
| "We are pleased to announce"    | "Great news" or "Guess what"                                        |
| "At this time"                  | "Right now"                                                         |
| "Moving forward"                | "From here" or "Next up"                                            |
| "Stakeholders"                  | "Neighbors" or "Everyone involved"                                  |
| "Utilize"                       | "Use"                                                               |
| "Facilitate"                    | "Help" or "Run" or "Set up"                                         |
| "Engagement"                    | "Getting involved" or "Showing up"                                  |
| "Infrastructure"                | Name the actual thing — "sidewalks," "streetlights," "storm drains" |
| "Initiative"                    | "Project" or "effort" or just say what it is                        |
| "Per our bylaws"                | "Here's how it works"                                               |
| "Quorum"                        | "Enough of us"                                                      |
| "Motion to approve"             | "We agreed to"                                                      |
| "Action items"                  | "Next steps" or "Here's what's happening"                           |
| "Disseminate information"       | "Spread the word" or "Let people know"                              |
| "Fiscal year"                   | "This year"                                                         |
| "Pursuant to"                   | Just cut it entirely                                                |

## Brand Rules

These are non-negotiable. Every piece of content follows them.

1. **Prefer "Sprecher East" in public-facing copy.** Never abbreviate to "SENA" or "SE." In legal/bylaw contexts, "the Association" is acceptable where formal wording requires it.

2. **Always disclose AI assistance.** We use AI tools to help build and maintain this platform. We say so openly. A simple line like "Built with AI assistance" or "This content was drafted with AI help and reviewed by neighbors" is enough. No hiding it.

3. **Always include unofficial status when relevant.** We are a grassroots, unofficial neighborhood group — not a government body, not a registered nonprofit (yet). When we're communicating externally or someone might mistake us for an official entity, we say so.

4. **Source all factual claims.** If we cite a number, a date, or a city decision, we link to or name the source. "The city said" is not good enough. "The city's 2025 Capital Budget report shows" is.

5. **8th grade reading level max.** Run important content through a readability checker. If a 14-year-old can't understand it, simplify it. This is a neighborhood website, not an academic journal.

6. **One clear call to action per page or post.** Don't overwhelm people with five things to do. Pick the most important one and make it obvious.

7. **Keep it current.** Stale content is worse than no content. If an event has passed, archive it. If info has changed, update it. A website stuck in 2024 tells neighbors nobody's home.
